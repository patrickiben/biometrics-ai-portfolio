Bringing routine biostatistics programming in-house with AI

One question, answered across every artifact below: can we bring routine biostatistics programming in-house and do it faster, more consistently, and more defensibly — with AI we control? The recommendation is a hybrid stack (Claude for capability + an on-prem model for sovereignty and reproducibility), with humans and validated tools always in the loop.

Scope & housekeeping. This home indexes the company biometrics-AI program only. Older duplicate package snapshots were moved to _archive/, and separate personal research was moved to _personal/ — neither is part of this program.
Guardrails (apply throughout): operations-only — no PHI, no participant-level data; reported/regulated numbers come only from validated tools (Phoenix WinNonlin, Pinnacle 21, EDC/CTMS), never an LLM; every draft is human-signed; the programming team is an in-house capability. Value framed as capacity, cycle-time, quality, and sovereignty — not budget or headcount. Most artifacts are AI-built templates and proofs-of-capability that jump-start validated builds, not validated production systems.
