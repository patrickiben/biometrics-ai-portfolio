# Risk Signals

Catalogs the predefined safety and conduct signals that trigger escalation in CP-101.

- **Governs:** the signal definitions and thresholds-as-methods (e.g., categories of laboratory shift, vital-sign change, AE clustering by cohort, and protocol-deviation patterns) plus the routing rules that move each signal to the right reviewer. This note specifies *what* is watched and *how* it is flagged — never the observed values themselves.
- **Owner:** clinical safety physician owns the medical criteria; biostatistics owns the detection logic and the blinded flagging outputs.
- **Connections:** signals are surfaced through the cadence set in the [[Monitoring Plan]] and, when a threshold is met, packaged for review under [[DSMB Operations]].

Definitions are frozen ahead of first dose so flagging stays consistent across cohorts and dose levels.
