① How our team works

The single most important thing to understand on day one — and the thing most easily misread:

  • Statistical programming is an in-house programming team (not outsourced). Years ago the company relocated programming in-house; it is our team, in‑house, under our processes.
  • The accountable statisticians own the science, the specs, and the QC, and sign every output. You (NA) own the science, the specs, the QC, and you sign every output. Programming is handled by the in-house team.
  • Work runs through a ~12‑hour coordination loop. A question asked here in the afternoon is answered the next morning. That handoff is the rhythm of the job (and the friction the AI proposal is trying to reduce).
  • Blinded vs unblinded roles are real and enforced. Most of the team — including you, by default — works blinded. Unblinded data (treatment assignments, randomization, certain safety/PK) is walled off to specific unblinded roles (an unblinded statistician/pharmacist, the IVRS/randomization system, safety/PV). If you're blinded, you must not seek or handle unblinded data, and you keep it out of any shared tool.
Why this matters for everything else: because programming is in‑house and you are the signer, you are accountable for what ships — which is why the rules below are non‑negotiable, and why AI here only ever drafts behind a human and validated tools.

② Your rules of the road

These are not "what makes the program safe to adopt." They are the rules you personally follow, from day one:

Reported or regulated numbers come ONLY from validated tools — Phoenix WinNonlin (PK/NCA), Pinnacle 21 (CDISC conformance), the EDC/CTMS, the validated SAS/R pipeline. A number is never sourced from an LLM, a Knowledge‑Base note, or a draft. AI may draft words around the numbers; it never produces the numbers.
Operations content only in any AI/shared tool — never PHI, never participant‑level data, never unblinded assignments or randomization, never sponsor‑restricted IP. If you're unsure whether something is operational, treat it as restricted.
RuleWhat it means for you
Say "participant," not "subject"In all prose/deliverables. But keep CDISC variables exactly — USUBJID, SUBJID never change.
Blinding integrityIf you're blinded, don't seek, view, or route unblinded data. Keep it out of the Study KB and any shared/AI tool.
Independent double‑programmingKey derivations/outputs are produced twice, independently, and reconciled — that's how we trust a number, not because one program ran.
A qualified human signs every outputAI and automation draft and check; a named biostatistician reviews, signs, and is accountable. Nothing ships on a tool's say‑so.
Cloud AI needs the compliant pathConsumer "Claude/ChatGPT" plans are not HIPAA‑eligible. The compliant route is the API under a signed BAA — and in practice PHI never goes to the cloud at all.

③ What's real today vs proposed

So you never mistake a polished proposal for current practice:

◷ Proposed — pending approval, NOT in production

The AI program being pitched to leadership

  • The hybrid AI stack (cloud Claude + on‑prem local model behind one gateway), the human‑approval queue, the phased pilot — a recommendation, not today's process.
  • The polished operations wikis (e.g. the 75‑automation hybrid platform, the SLM operating wikis) describe a proposed future state.

A simple test: if a thing would put a number or a regulated output into the world, it goes through the validated path and a human signer — never an AI tool, no matter how finished the demo looks.

⑤ Glossary — the acronyms you'll hit

TLF — Tables, Listings & Figures: the statistical outputs of a study.
SDTM / ADaM — CDISC standards: SDTM = collected data; ADaM = analysis‑ready datasets derived from it.
NCA — Non‑Compartmental Analysis: the standard PK parameter derivation (Cmax, AUC, t½…), done in Phoenix WinNonlin.
Phoenix WinNonlin — the validated PK/NCA tool of record.
Pinnacle 21 — the validated CDISC (SDTM/ADaM) conformance checker.
pharmaverse — open R packages for clinical work; admiral (ADaM) + tern/rtables (TLFs).
DB‑lock / DBL — Database Lock: the data is frozen for analysis.
USUBJID / SUBJID — CDISC participant identifiers (keep the variable names exactly).
DLT — Dose‑Limiting Toxicity (dose‑escalation safety).
SRC / DSMB — Safety Review Committee / Data Safety Monitoring Board — the bodies that decide on safety signals.
QTL / KRI — Quality Tolerance Limit / Key Risk Indicator (risk‑based monitoring thresholds).
eDISH / Hy's Law — liver‑safety screening views (note: not used in our TLF libraries).
BLQ / LLOQ — Below Limit of Quantification / Lower Limit (PK concentration handling).
BAA — Business Associate Agreement: the contract that makes a cloud service HIPAA‑eligible.
PHI — Protected Health Information (never goes in a shared/AI tool).
eTMF / RBQM — electronic Trial Master File / Risk‑Based Quality Management (operations).
SLM / GGUF — Small Language Model / its on‑disk file format (the on‑prem model).
CSR — Clinical Study Report: the end of the Protocol→CSR pipeline.
